# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International short-term guests staying at 136 Marine Parade, Southport (two units: Aqualine and Unit 21/Unit 1). They open the guide on their phone, often mid-trip, in one of seven languages, to find check-in codes, Wi-Fi, house rules, appliance instructions, and nearby recommendations. Low patience for friction: they want the answer, then to put the phone down and get back to their holiday.

## Product Purpose

A digital guest-guide microsite that replaces (and outperforms) a laminated welcome folder: check-in/access instructions gated behind a per-booking code, house info, and local recommendations, in the guest's own language. Success looks like a guest finding what they need in one glance, in the right language, without feeling like they're wrestling a web app.

## Brand Personality

Warm, refined, reassuring. Boutique hospitality, not corporate SaaS. Voice is calm and hosts-not-hoteliers; visually it reads like a well-produced printed welcome booklet (Fraunces serif headings, warm cream paper, navy ink, teal + amber accents) rather than a dashboard or listing-site template.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS/OTA chrome: no default unstyled browser form controls sitting undressed in the layout, no Airbnb/Booking.com-style utilitarian listing-site UI, no dashboard-y select boxes or dense app furniture. The guide should feel considered on every surface, including small controls like the language switcher.

## Design Principles

- **Every control earns its polish.** Small, frequently-used elements (language switcher, Wi-Fi reveal, QR modal) get the same craft as the hero — nothing defaults to raw browser chrome.
- **Language switching is a first-class feature, not an afterthought.** Guests actively rely on it; it must visibly confirm the switch happened and read as an intentional part of the design, not a bolted-on utility.
- **Mobile-first, glance-first.** Guests are mid-trip on a phone. Controls need large enough touch targets and immediate legibility.
- **Quiet confidence over decoration.** Warmth and refinement come from restraint (paper texture, serif display type, teal/amber used sparingly) — not from adding visual noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Multilingual by design (7 languages, CJK font fallback already handled in CSS). Keyboard and screen-reader accessible controls are required throughout, since guests may rely on assistive tech while traveling. No stated formal WCAG level; treat WCAG 2.1 AA as the working bar (focus-visible states, sufficient contrast, accessible labels).
